New World screwworm — the feed and mill operations angle is uncovered
USDA has sought a FIFRA pesticide exemption and FDA authorized emergency use of F10 ointment in response to New World screwworm. Policy and veterinary titles are covering the disease and regulatory response. Feed Strategy has a dedicated biosecurity section and no screwworm coverage despite this being an active USDA emergency. Screwworm outbreaks disrupt livestock movement in affected regions — directly affecting feed demand patterns, procurement timelines, and mill planning.
Editorial angle
What does a screwworm outbreak mean operationally for feed manufacturers? Procurement, inventory buffers, customer communication. That thread is unwritten and it's Feed Strategy's distinctive lens.
Audience signal
Differentiates Feed Strategy from titles covering the same story from policy and veterinary angles only.
