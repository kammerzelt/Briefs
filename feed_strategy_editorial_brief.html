The American Feed Industry Association sent a formal letter to President Trump flagging food security risks from this concentration. With tariffs active and escalating, this has moved from a strategic concern to an operational problem for every feed manufacturer in your readership. New World screwworm — the feed and mill operations angle is uncovered
USDA has sought a FIFRA pesticide exemption and FDA authorized emergency use of F10 ointment in response to New World screwworm. Other trade titles have covered this from the policy and veterinary angles. Feed Strategy has a dedicated biosecurity section and no screwworm coverage. Screwworm outbreaks disrupt livestock movement in affected regions — which directly affects feed demand patterns, ingredient procurement timelines, and mill planning for manufacturers serving those markets.
Coverage gap
What does a screwworm outbreak scenario mean operationally for feed manufacturers — procurement, inventory buffers, customer communication planning? That thread has not been written and it is Feed Strategy's distinctive lens applied to a story everyone else is covering from a different angle.

Alltech 2026 Agri-Food Outlook — the 2026 edition has not been covered in your archive
Alltech released its 15th annual Agri-Food Outlook with data from 38,837 feed mills across 142 countries — reporting 1.44 billion metric tons of global feed production, up 2.9%, with China growing 4.8% driven by a structural shift from on-farm to commercial feed. Feed Strategy has covered past Alltech Outlook editions and owns the WATT Top Feed Companies franchise. The 2026 version has been picked up by commodity and dairy titles but a Feed Strategy feature anchored to the manufacturing and formulation implications has not yet appeared in your archive.
Story angle
The most actionable insight is structural: China's growth isn't more animals — it's commercial feed replacing on-farm mixing. What does that shift mean for global ingredient demand, trade flows, and competitive dynamics for North American manufacturers? That's a Feed Strategy story.

FEED Act — 2026 status update warranted on legislation Feed Strategy already owns
Feed Strategy covered the FEED Act's introduction at the House level in 2024. The legislation — which would allow feed ingredients to claim production efficiency and animal health benefits, closing the competitive gap with EU, South American, and Australian manufacturers — has been a stated NGFA and AFIA priority for 2026 with bipartisan Congressional support. No 2026 status and implications piece exists in your archive.
Coverage angle
A 2026 update — where the legislation stands, what passage would mean practically for formulators, what the timeline looks like — serves your regulatory-watching readership and builds on a story you already own rather than starting from scratch.
