What's Rising
Search clusters gaining the most momentum right now.
Act Now
+340% YoY Cold Plunge & Contrast Therapy · 12-month sustained climb
Homeowners aren't just curious about cold plunge anymore — they're ready to buy. And they're asking builders and retailers who aren't ready to answer.
Cold plunge and contrast therapy searches have tripled over the past 12 months. This isn't a spike driven by a single viral moment — it's a sustained climb that's still accelerating. The search pattern mirrors what happened with outdoor kitchens in 2018 and swim spas in 2020: a consumer behavior shift that the trade is always 12 to 18 months behind. The customers walking into your showroom or calling for a quote have already done their research. The question is whether you're ready to have the conversation.
What to do this month
If cold plunge isn't part of your standard quote conversation, add it. Builders: position it as a natural add alongside spa or hot tub. Retailers: know your product options before a customer walks in knowing exactly what they want. Service pros: contrast therapy installations need ongoing chemistry management — that's a recurring service conversation.

+67% YoY Robotic Pool Cleaners · Pre-season buying pattern
Robotic cleaner searches peak before pool season opens — the buying decision happens now, not when the pool is already green.
Searches for robotic pool cleaners follow a predictable pattern: they rise sharply in April and May as homeowners prepare to open, then plateau through summer. Right now is the highest-intent moment of the buying cycle — the consumer is actively researching before the need becomes urgent. This year's pre-season volume is up 67% over last May, suggesting a larger cohort of first-time buyers entering the category, likely people who purchased pools during the 2020 to 2022 boom and are now investing in maintenance equipment.
What to do this month
Retailers: floor space and staff knowledge for robotic cleaners needs to be ready now, not mid-June. If your team can't confidently compare the top three models, that sale goes to Amazon. Service pros: proactively recommending robotic cleaners to your established accounts is a revenue conversation you own — the customer is already researching it independently.

+45% YoY Pool Automation Systems · Sustained 3-year build
Pool automation has been climbing for three years. It's no longer a luxury feature homeowners ask about — it's what they expect when they call you.
The automation trend is driven by two things happening simultaneously: rising electricity costs making scheduled pump operation financially meaningful, and a generation of pool owners who consider smartphone control a baseline expectation rather than an upgrade. Searches for "pool control app" and "smart pool system" are at all-time highs. The homeowner who just bought a pool in 2021 is now renovating their equipment pad — and automation is at the top of that list.
What to do this month
Builders: if automation isn't a standard package option in your proposals, you're behind where your customers' expectations are. Service pros: retrofitting automation on existing equipment pads is a strong upsell for spring service calls — lead with the energy savings, close with the convenience.

+38% YoY Fiberglass Pool Cost & Installation · "Fiberglass vs gunite" at 5-year high
More homeowners are choosing fiberglass over gunite than at any point in the last five years. The comparison search is how they're making that decision.
The "fiberglass vs gunite" search query is at its highest volume in five years — which means a large portion of consumers currently planning pool projects haven't decided on construction type yet and are actively researching. Fiberglass is winning that comparison for a specific type of buyer: value-conscious, maintenance-aware, and timeline-sensitive. Builders who can speak fluently to that comparison — and who have the fiberglass product to back it — are capturing a growing share of first-call conversations.
What to do this month
Builders: the comparison conversation is happening before your first sales call. Having a clear, honest answer to "fiberglass vs gunite" for your specific market conditions — installation timeline, local soil, long-term maintenance — is the table stakes for the current buyer cohort.

What's Fading
Demand shifts that signal where attention has moved on.
Heads Up
−23% YoY Above Ground Pools · Pandemic boom fully unwound
The above-ground pool surge is over. The buyer who entered the market in 2020 has either upgraded or moved on.
Above-ground pool searches peaked in summer 2021 and have declined every year since. The 2020 to 2022 cohort of pandemic buyers was a historically unusual demand spike — not a structural shift in the market. The good news for the trade: many of those buyers are now three to four years into pool ownership and entering the renovation and equipment upgrade cycle. The product they bought has changed; the customer relationship doesn't have to.
What to watch
Retailers holding significant above-ground inventory are swimming against a demand current. The conversation to have with your 2021 above-ground customers is now about liners, covers, pumps, and chemistry — not new pools. Service companies that added above-ground accounts during the boom should be deepening those relationships before a competitor does.
Retailers Service
−31% YoY Inflatable & Entry-Level Portable Spas · Demand concentration moving upmarket
Entry-level spa demand is declining — but premium portable spa and contrast therapy demand is replacing it. The spa buyer has moved upmarket.
Inflatable spa and low-cost portable hot tub searches are down sharply from their 2021 peak. What's growing is searches for quality portable spas, contrast therapy installations, and sauna-plus-cold-plunge combinations — the wellness-oriented, higher-investment spa purchase. The customer hasn't left the category. They've graduated within it. The trade opportunity is following them there.
What to watch
Retailers who built spa inventory around entry-price product are facing the wrong end of a demand shift. The contrast therapy and premium spa buyer has a larger budget and a more considered purchase process — they respond to expertise and trust more than price. That's the customer to build toward.
